INTRODUCTION 
Background 
 In the rapidly evolving digital era, Electronic Governance (e -Governance) has 
emerged as a global standard for modern public administration. Across the world, 
governments are transitioning from traditional, paper-based bureaucracies to sophisticated 
digital platforms to enhance transparency, accountability, and citizen engagement. This 
shift is driven by the Fourth Industrial Revolution, where data is considered the new oil, 
and the ability to process information instantly is synonymous with effective gover nance 
[1]. The integration of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) in government 
operations allows for faster data retrieval, secure record -keeping, and improved 
responsiveness to public needs, ensuring that no community is left behind in the glo bal 
march toward digitalization. 
 In the context of the Philippines, the push for digital transformation is not merely a 
trend but a legislative mandate. The passage of Republic Act 11032, also known as the 
"Ease of Doing Business and Efficient Government Service Delivery Act of 2018," 
compels all government units to streamline their procedures and adopt automated systems 
to reduce paperwork [2]. As an archipelagic nation, the Philippines relies heavily on its 
Local Government Units (LGUs) to deliver essential services to the grassroots le vel. The 
Barangay, defined as the smallest political unit in the country, serves as the primary 
planning and implementing unit of government policies, plans, programs, projects, and

activities in the community [3]. It is the first line of defense in maintaining peace and order 
and the primary point of contact for citizens seeking government assistance. 
 However, despite these national mandates and the critical role they play, a 
significant "digital divide" persists. While major cities in the Philippines have begun 
adopting "Smart City" technologies, many barangays especially those in provincial and far-
flung areas remain tied to obsolete administrative practices. A vast number of these units 
still lack the necessary financial resources and technical infrastructure to modernize, 
forcing them to rely on manual handwritten logbooks, physical filing cabinets,  and 
fragmented Excel spreadsheets to manage vital crucial data [4]. These traditional methods 
are come with risks: paper records are vulnerable to physical damage from natural disasters 
such as floods or fires, they are prone to human error and redundancy, and they make data 
retrieval a time-consuming, labor-intensive process that slow down decision-making. 
 Locally, this struggle is evident in Barangay Pakiad, a community in the 
Municipality of Oton, Iloilo. Currently, the barangay administration operates without a 
centralized information system. The officials rely on manual record-keeping for managing 
resident profiles, processing business permits, and issuing clearances. This lack of 
digitization creates a bottleneck in service delivery; simple requests for documents can take 
longer than necessary due to the time spent searching through physical piles of pa per. 
Furthermore, the absence of a secure, digital database raises concerns regarding data 
privacy and security, as physical logbooks can be easily misplaced or accessed by 
unauthorized personnel.

 The problem is further compounded by the lack of a modern mechanism for 
community engagement, particularly in incident reporting. In times of emergencies or 
community disputes, residents often have no choice but to physically visit the barangay 
hall or rely on unverified channels to report issues. This delay in communication can have

 To address these multifaceted inefficiencies, the researchers propose the 
development of "CommunaLink: Online Barangay Management System."  This 
project aims to bridge the technological gap in Barangay Pakiad by introducing a 
centralized, cloud -based platform. By migrating from manual logs to a secure Google

administrative workflows. Moreover, the integration of an Incident Reporting System using 
geolocation technology will empower residents to participate actively in community safety, 
creating a governance model that is not only efficient and transparent but also responsive 
to the digital needs of the modern Filipino [5].

"CommunaLink" is to bridge this digital divide by providing a centralized platform that

Scope and Delimitations of the Study 
            This project’s scope includes developing and implementing an “Online Barangay 
Management System” using Google Cloud’s infrastructure and Google Cloud SQL for 
database. The system will perform administrative tasks including Create, Read, Update,  
Delete (CRUD) operations to manage data for the automated generation of the barangay’s 
official documents. The system will be optimized for flexible access. The system will run 
using web browsers with intuitive and responsive design that is optimized for both user 
interfaces for administrators, (via desktop) and residents (via mobile web app) through the 
incident reporting feature. 
           The Incident reporting system is designed to assist requests and expedite response 
through geolocation mapping. This feature allows residents to swiftly note and record 
"what happened," "when," and "where," along with multimedia attachments. Residents (via 
mobile web app) and barangay administrators (via desktop), will have different interfaces 
and access levels in the system. Residents can have the ability to submit new reports, 
request for documents and monitor their current status, while admi nistrators can view, 
manage requests, and analyze incident reports to enable effective dispatch and strategic 
planning. To ensure accurate and dependable incident records, validation procedures will 
be implemented to verify the integrity of submitted data. 
            As part of the project, a simple web -based Graphical User Interface (GUI) will be 
developed to interact with the system and allow for the barangay administrators to access

Review of Related Literature 
            Traditional manual methods, which mostly rely on handwritten logs, physical filing 
systems and unconventional use of software tools like Microsoft’s Word, and Excel for

disadvantages in preserving official documents, property records and profiles. In response 
to these challenges various initiatives for the impro vement of barangays have been 
developed.

Local Studies 
Enhancing Local Governance and Disaster Resilience through a Data -Driven

Barangay Constituents Information and Services Management System (BCISMS) 
            BCISMS or The Barangay Constituents Information and Services Management 
System is a centralized digital platform designed to address the significant inefficiencies 
of manual record -keeping in local barangay operations whose traditional paper -based 
methods for issuing certificates and managing resident data are slow and prone to errors 
often leading to record redundancy and long transaction periods. To create a tool that 
enhances the speed and reliability of administrative tasks, and improving th e delivery of 
services to the community. BCISMS features, rapid record searching, health information 
management, automated report generation, and graphical analysis of the barangay census.  
            The system followed an Iterative Development Model, a process that involves 
building and refining the software in repeated cycles. Officials from five barangays of 
Talisay City were interviewed to ground the layout of the study. The system was tested by 
two distinct groups using internationally recognized standards: IT experts evaluated its 
technical quality using McCall's Software Quality Model, while barangay officials judged 
its practical use against the ISO/IEC 9126 -1:2000 criteria, meeting a comprehensive 
validation [12].

 The reviewed foreign and local studies share a common goal: to leverage 
technological advancement to overcome the inefficiencies of traditional, manual processes 
in community governance. Both demonstrate a clear trend toward digitization, improving 
service delivery, enhancing public safety, and promoting community engagement. 
However, they differ in their primary focus and technological application. 
 The foreign studies emphasize the development of advanced, real -time safety 
systems designed for critical incident responses. The Neighbourhood Alert System utilizes 
high-tech solutions with Natural Language Processing to intelligently classify user reports, 
while the WSU -Alert system provides "dual -action" features for immediate emergency

 This study employs a descriptive research method to systematically assess the 
effectiveness and usability of the developed "CommunaLink" system. The researchers

designed to gather numerical feedback from the respondents. This approach allows for 
the objective measurement of the system's performance in terms of functionality, 
efficiency, and user satisfaction. The assessment will be conducted by the intended end-
users specifically the barangay officials and residents whose responses will be quantified 
to determine if the developed application successfully meets the operational standards 
and requirements of Barangay Pakiad.

Deployment 
 The system will be installed and set up in the target environment, after which 
the system is officially launched. 
Before the system will be available, several steps will be completed: 
• Environment Setup: Google Cloud Platform integration will be finalized. This 
includes the Google App Engine configuration for the web server, the Google 
Cloud SQL for the database and the Google Cloud Storage for multimedia file 
handling. 
• Data Initialization: Essential data will be loaded into the system with 
coordination with the barangay officials. This includes admin accounts for the 
barangay officials, bulk loading of the resident data, if possible, to make the 
system ready for deployment. 
• Final User Acceptance Testing (UAT) Sign-off: The selected participants of the 
testing will provide a final sign-off, confirming that the system meets the quality, 
standards, and requirements needed for operational use. 
The system will be launched using a phased rollout to manage the transition effectively:

• Internal System Rollout: This rollout will be deployed first to the barangay

generation, user features before the resident interaction begins. 
• Public System Launch: Following the successful internal rollout, Incident 
Reporting System will be launched to the residents. This can be announced via 
community notice board, broadcast messaging through their emails. The 
Residents will be guided on how to register and use the web-app for document 
requests and incident filing. 
• Training: A training session will be conducted for the barangay to ensure they are 
proficient with all the features and functions of the application. The residents will 
be given simple user guides and materials for easy adoption. 
Post-Deployment Support: 
 Extensive support will be available for the barangay after the official launching 
for a period of two to four weeks. This will provide support to any misunderstandings, 
unforeseen issues, to ensure the system’s stability. The researchers will actively monitor 
the system’s performance and logs during this phase to adhere to any concern.

Iterative Cycle 
The Improvement process will follow a structured cycle: 
1. Feedback Collection: Surveys will be gathered from both barangay officials and 
residents through a live form and through a built-in form in the system.  
2. Analysis and Prioritization: At the end of each month, the researchers will 
review collected feedback, issues and suggestions. These will be categorized 
based on their content and urgency on the system. 
3. Planning and Development: Based on the categorized list, the researchers will 
plan the next step for enhancements This could range from minor bug fixes, minor 
tweaks to the interface to adding new features. 
4. Testing and Deployment of Updates: Any change will be tested and will be 
thoroughly addressed in a different environment platform before deploying to the 
main server. This ensures that updates will not introduce new problems the 
system. Cycle repeats the process to continually adapt to the changing needs of 
the barangay.

System Testing:  
• How it will be conducted:  A Comprehensive testing will involve executing 
end-to-end scenarios. This will simulate real -world usage, covering all 
functional requirements, testing the performance under expected load, testing 
security vulnerabilities, and overall usability. Testing w ill be done by the 
researchers along with dedicated testers.

requirements.  
User Acceptance Testing (UAT):  
• How it will be conducted: The last phase of testing will include real users.  
o Respondents: Selected Barangay officials for the Barangay 
Management System, and a selected representative group of residents 
for the Incident reporting system will be asked.  
o Methodology: Users will be walked through the whole system after 
that they will be asked to do test cases that reflect their daily tasks and 
typical scenarios. They will also perform free -form testing to explore 
the system as they see fit.  
o Feedback Collection: Questionnaires will be handed to them after the 
testing. The questionnaires will include Likert scale for satisfaction and

Evaluation  
 Evaluation is dedicated to systematically assessing the overall success, 
effectiveness, efficiency, and user satisfaction of the Barangay Management and 
Incident Reporting System.  
Evaluation Process:  
The evaluation process will focus on a multi-dimensional assessment, aiming to:  
• Verify Usability: Determine how user friendly and intuitive the system is for 
Barangay officials and residents.  
• Verify Functionality: To confirm that all implemented features and 
functionalities work correctly and meet the requirements.  
• Measuring Efficiency: Evaluate the system’s effectiveness compared to 
previous methods.  
• User Satisfaction: To measure the level of satisfaction among both 
administrative users and residents with their experience on the system.  
• Determine Impact: Analyze the system's impact on data integrity, enhancing 
communication, increasing community responsiveness, and contributing to 
more effective local governance in the Barangay.

Data Presentation and Analysis 
          The data collected during the User Acceptance Testing (UAT) phase will be 
analyzed using both quantitative and qualitative methods to evaluate the system's 
usability, functionality, efficiency, and user satisfaction. 
• Quantitative Analysis: Data from the Likert scale questions on the survey 
questionnaires will be analyzed using descriptive statistics. Mean scores, standard 
deviations, and frequency distributions will be calculated for each criterion (e.g., 
usability, functionality, satisfaction) for both Barangay Officials/Staff and 
Resident respondent groups. This will provide measurable insights into user 
perceptions and system performance. Spreadsheet software will be used for these 
calculations. 
• Qualitative Analysis: Open-ended comments and suggestions gathered from the 
questionnaires and potentially during testing observations will undergo thematic 
analysis. This process involves identifying, coding, and categorizing recurring 
themes, patterns, and specific points of feedback related to user experience, 
system strengths, weaknesses, and areas for improvement. This analysis will 
provide richer context to the quantitative findings and inform future iterations of 
the system table.

Ethical Considerations  
 The researchers are committed to upholding the highest ethical standards. The 
following considerations have been integrated into the research protocol in 
compliance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012: 
• Voluntary Participation:  Participation is entirely voluntary. Respondents 
have the right to withdraw at any time without penalty. 
• Informed Consent:  A "Certificate of Consent" will be provided to all 
participants. The Informed Consent Form and Questionnaires have been 
translated into the local dialect (Hiligaynon/Kinaray -a) for improved 
comprehension. 
• Privacy and Confidentiality: Strict confidentiality will be observed. All data 
collected (names, survey responses) will be anonymized and stored securely 
in the Google Cloud SQL database with restricted access. Research data such 
as survey responses and feedbacks forms will be stored in password-protected 
cloud storage (Google Drive) or encrypted local storage, accessible only to the 
researchers. All data will be used solely for this study and will be permanently 
shredded or deleted upon the project’s completion.

Appendix E: 
Definition of Terms 
For better understanding of the study, the following terms are defined conceptually and 
operationally: 
• Barangay: The smallest administrative division in the Philippines, serving as the 
primary political and governance unit at the community level. Barangays are 
responsible for the implementation of government policies, delivering services 
near to the community and maintaining public order within their jurisdiction [1]. 
• Barangay Incident Reporting System: The proposed online web application 
integration for citizen-initiated incident reporting. This citizen-centered system 
enhances the barangay's response to emergencies by utilizing new and mature 
technologies which supports better documentation and tracking of community 
issues. 
• Barangay Management System: Online web application component, designed 
for administrative data management. This component handles all the essential 
tasks for the barangay to function properly, generating official documents, 
clearances, certificates, and organizing internal records which reduces paperwork 
by digitization. 
• CommunaLink: The title of the proposed online web application that combines 
barangay data management and incident reports in one platform. It is designed for

barangay admins to carry out their everyday tasks and for residents to create a 
more connected, efficient, and responsive local governance system. 
• CRUD (Create, Read, Update, Delete): Fundamental operations for persistent 
storage, allowing users to manage data records [21]. Admins should be able to 
manage resident profiles, business information, and official documents via CRUD 
functions. For the Incident Reporting System, it allows the creation and retrieval 
of incident reports with status updates. 
• Electronic Governance (e-Governance): The application of IT for delivering 
government services. Operationally, it refers to the shift from manual logbooks to 
the web-based platform proposed in this study [22]. 
• Geolocation: The identification of the real-world geographic location of an 
object, such as a mobile phone or internet-connected computer [23]. The Incident 
Reporting System heavily relies on geolocation which enables residents to pin a 
location identifying the incident. The barangay uses this capability to enhance 
response and planning.
